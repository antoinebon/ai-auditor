INFORMATION SECURITY POLICY — EXCERPT

1. Purpose and Scope
This policy establishes the framework for protecting the confidentiality,
integrity, and availability of organizational information assets. It applies
to all employees, contractors, consultants, and third parties who access
organizational information or systems.

2. Information Security Management System
The organization maintains an Information Security Management System (ISMS)
aligned with ISO 27001. The ISMS is owned by the Chief Information Security
Officer and reviewed by executive leadership at least quarterly. Policies
are reviewed annually and following any significant change to the business,
regulatory environment, or threat landscape.

3. Roles and Responsibilities
The CISO owns the ISMS and reports to the CEO. Asset owners are accountable
for the security of their systems and data. The Security Operations team
operates controls and responds to incidents. All employees are responsible
for following the policies and reporting suspected issues.

4. Information Classification
Information is classified into four tiers: Public, Internal, Confidential,
and Restricted. Handling requirements for each tier are defined in the
Information Handling Standard, including labelling, storage, transmission,
retention, and disposal expectations.

5. Access Control
Access to information systems is granted based on role and the principle of
least privilege. Access requests are submitted through the access management
platform and approved by the employee's manager and the system owner.
Access rights are reviewed every 90 days by asset owners; dormant accounts
are disabled after 30 days of inactivity. Privileged access is granted only
to individuals with a documented operational need and is logged and reviewed
monthly.

6. Authentication
All users authenticate with single sign-on and multi-factor authentication
wherever supported. Passwords must meet minimum complexity and length
requirements defined in the Password Standard. Shared accounts are
prohibited except where explicitly approved by the CISO with compensating
controls.

7. Asset Management
An inventory of information assets is maintained in the asset management
system. Each asset has an assigned owner, classification level, and
applicable controls. Asset inventory is reconciled quarterly with the
configuration management database.

8. Cryptography
Cryptographic controls protect confidential and restricted information in
transit and at rest. Approved algorithms and key lengths are documented in
the Cryptography Standard. Key management follows the Key Management
Procedure, including periodic rotation and protected storage.

9. Physical Security
Office facilities use layered perimeter controls including access-card entry,
visitor sign-in, and CCTV monitoring. Server rooms require dual-factor entry
and are monitored continuously. Visitors are escorted at all times in
restricted areas.

10. Training and Awareness
All personnel complete information security awareness training on hire and
annually thereafter. Role-specific training is provided for developers,
system administrators, and privileged users. Phishing simulations are
conducted quarterly, and results inform additional training.

11. Supplier Security
Suppliers with access to information assets are subject to the Supplier
Security Program. This includes risk assessment before onboarding,
contractual security requirements, and ongoing monitoring. Annual reviews
cover supplier compliance with agreed requirements.

12. Incident Management
The organization maintains an Information Security Incident Response Plan.
Incidents are detected through monitoring, user reports, and threat
intelligence. The plan defines severity levels, escalation paths, and
communication expectations. The Security Incident Response Team includes
the CISO, Legal, Communications, and Engineering leads. Post-incident
reviews are conducted for every incident classified Major or above, and
lessons learned are tracked to closure.

13. Business Continuity and ICT Resilience
Critical business processes are supported by documented continuity plans.
ICT recovery objectives are defined per service in the Business Impact
Analysis. Backups are encrypted, replicated off-site, and tested quarterly
through restore exercises.

14. Vulnerability and Patch Management
Vulnerability scans are performed weekly against production systems. Patch
timelines are based on severity and asset criticality, with critical
vulnerabilities remediated within 14 days of disclosure. Exceptions are
documented and reviewed by the CISO.

15. Logging and Monitoring
Security-relevant events are logged centrally and retained for at least 12
months. The Security Operations Centre monitors logs for anomalies 24/7 and
responds per the Incident Response Plan. Log integrity is protected through
write-once storage.

16. Policy Compliance and Exceptions
Compliance with this policy is verified through internal audits, control
testing, and management reviews. Exceptions must be formally requested,
risk-assessed, and approved by the CISO with a defined expiry date.
